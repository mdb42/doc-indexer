The conference began at St. Paul's Cathedral in London, England, before moving to the Louvre Museum in Paris, France. Delegates traveled from New York City, NY to San Francisco-Oakland Bay Area, stopping at Chicago's O'Hare International Airport and Washington D.C.'s Union Station. The itinerary included visits to the Great Wall of China near Beijing, the Taj Mahal in Agra, India, and the ancient ruins of Machu Picchu, Peru. Research presentations were held at Oxford University's Bodleian Library, Harvard Medical School, and the Max-Planck Institute in Munich, Germany. Field studies took place in South-East Asia, ranging from Hong Kong SAR to Ho Chi Minh City, while archaeological excavations continued at Giza, Egypt and Petra, Jordan. The final symposium was hosted at Mount Sinai Medical Center on New York's Upper East Side, with satellite events at MIT-Harvard Center in Cambridge, Massachusetts and Stanford University in Palo Alto, California.
